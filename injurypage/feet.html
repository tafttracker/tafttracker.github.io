What Is Up With Your Feet?

Feet Ankles

Feet

Posterior Tibial Tendon Dysfunction(PTTD)
  • Causes:
    Overuse of the posterior tibial tendon, often due to activities that involve the tendon, such as running, walking, hiking, or climbing stairs.
  • Symptoms:
    Pain, swelling, a flattening of the arch, and an inward rolling of the ankle. As it becomes more advanced, the arch flattens even more and the pain often shifts to the outside of the foot, below the ankle.
  • Treatment:
    Nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, ankle brace, short-leg cast or boot
Plantar Fasciitis
  • Causes:
    Stretching of the tight bands of the plantar fascia which result in micro tears as they extend from the heel. Sudden weight gain.
  • Symptoms:
    Pain at the bottom of the heel. It is usually felt on the first step out of bed in the morning or when walking again after resting from walking or running. However, pain can, if it persists, be felt any time you are walking, running, or jumping.
  • Treatment:
    Taping, calf and plantar fascia stretches (with ample warm-up prior), massage, sports cream, night splint.
